Timeline of 2020

January
COVID-19 cases: 9,910; deaths: 213

This was the beginning of 2020. Notable events include the Australian bushfires, the eruption of Taal Volcano in the Philippines, and increasing tensions between Iran and the US.

February
COVID-19 cases: 85,850; deaths: 2,935

February was the calm before the storm. US politics slowly started to destablize, with President Trump being impeached by Congress. Meanwhile, the world mourns for Kobe Bryant.

March
COVID-19 cases: 770,136; deaths: 41,044

March was when COVID-19 was officially declared as a global pandemic. Lockdowns in all major countries were initiated. Cases skyrocketed, and the financial markets suffered one of the biggest losses in history.

April
COVID-19 cases: 3,106,067; deaths: 224,673

America experienced protests in the streets, demanding for lockdowns to be lifted. Meanwhile, China starts to ease quarantine restrictions.

May
COVID-19 cases: 5,941,191; deaths: 366,509

May began with the unfortunate death of George Floyd, which sparked riots and protests in American streets, the intensity of which increased as police response became abusive and violent.

June
COVID-19 cases: 10,176,371; deaths: 500,844

The Black Lives Matter situation in America sparked other countries to start their own protests against racial injustice. Statues of historically racist figures were toppled over by protesters.

July
COVID-19 cases: 17,110,285; deaths: 663,735

Bubonic plague scare in Mongolia.

The usual.

August
COVID-19 cases: 25,155,377; deaths: 844,881

The Beirut explosion defined the theme of this month. Flash flooding in Afghanistan, storms in the US and fires in California wreck the Earth.

September
COVID-19 cases: 33,568,622; deaths: 1,005,289

COVID-19 deaths reach 1 million, a sad yet historic milestone. Meanwhile, the political landscape of America becomes increasingly uncertain as the future of democracy is at risk.

October
COVID-19 cases: 39,944,915; deaths: 1,111,998

#PisayGiveUsABreak trends on Twitter. People worldwide hear the cries of the Pisayan people. Char 1/2. Protests against police brutality emerge in Hong Kong, Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, and other South East Asian countries.

November
COVID-19 cases: 53,840,548; deaths: 1,311,243

Biden wins the U.S. election. Trump plays the fraud card.


December
COVID-19 cases: 69,808,588; deaths: 1,576,986

Trump is extremely desperate. In other matters, rich countries are going to get the vaccine first. For poor countries, the timeline stretches until 2024. We truly live in a dystopian world.
